SIT

Voice Cue
"Sit" - said once, calm and clear tone. No repeating!
Body Language
Stand tall, treat hand slightly above dog's nose, move slowly upward. Wait for natural sit.
Timing
Command ‚Üí Wait 3-5 seconds ‚Üí Mark "Good!" instantly ‚Üí Reward
Reward
High-value treat + calm praise ("Good sit"). Keep energy low and steady.
üîÑ Regression Reset Tip
If ignoring "sit," go back to luring with treats for 3-5 reps, then fade the lure. Re-pattern, don't restart.

STAY

Voice Cue
"Stay" - firm but calm tone. Use open palm as visual cue.
Body Language
Open palm facing dog, step back slowly. Maintain eye contact. Start with 3 seconds.
Timing

Reward
Return to dog, pause, then reward. Don't call them‚Äîthis breaks the stay.
üîÑ Regression Reset Tip

COME

"Come!" - happy, excited tone. Make it sound like the best thing ever!

Reward

"Down" - calm, steady tone. Not demanding, just clear direction.
Body Language

üö® Emergency Reset Protocol

When Your Dog Is Completely Ignoring All Commands
  1. STOP REPEATING COMMANDS Don't say "sit, sit, SIT!" - this teaches them to ignore you.
  2. GO BACK TO TREATS Use high-value treats to lure the behavior for 5-10 reps.
  3. LOWER YOUR CRITERIA Reward any small movement toward the behavior you want.
  4. PRACTICE IN EASIER ENVIRONMENTS Move to a quieter space with fewer distractions.
  5. REBUILD GRADUALLY 2-3 days of easy success, then slowly add challenge back.
  6. STAY CALM Your frustration makes regression worse. Take breaks if needed.
Remember:

Regression isn't rebellion‚Äîit's your dog saying "I'm confused and need help rebuilding this pattern." Be their guide, not their opponent.
